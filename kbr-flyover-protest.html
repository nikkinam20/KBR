Why flyovers don't work

The hidden cost that nobody is talking about

  • Flyovers shift congestion, they don't solve it. Every flyover moves the bottleneck from one junction to the next. Hyderabad has built 40+ flyovers under SRDP. Congestion has worsened every year.
  • The pillars make the road below narrower. Where flyovers are built, the supporting columns eat into the road beneath. In many cases the reduced width can no longer accommodate full-sized buses — the vehicles that carry the most people.
  • The compensation mechanism makes congestion worse. When land is acquired for road widening, owners receive TDR — Transfer of Development Rights — allowing them to build extra floors on their rebuilt property. Around KBR Park alone, 327 properties are affected. Each rebuilt taller structure brings more residents, more vehicles, more parking demand. The new road capacity gets absorbed within years. The road was widened. The congestion returned. Nothing changed except the addresses.
  • This is a governance problem, not an infrastructure gap. Cars parked illegally along this corridor already consume an entire lane of road capacity at peak hours. Enforcing existing no-parking rules costs nothing and recovers that capacity immediately — no construction, no acquisition, no crores spent.

There is another way

What actually decongests cities

Cities comparable to Hyderabad in density and income have reduced congestion — not by building more roads, but by making existing roads work better. The tools are known. None require acquiring a single property.

1

Junction Redesign

Reconfigure signal timings, turning radii, and lane markings at all four junctions. Proven to reduce peak-hour delays by 20–40% at a fraction of flyover cost.

2

Dedicated Bus Lanes

A single bus carries 60–80 passengers. One dedicated bus lane on this corridor moves more people than two new flyover lanes — without displacing a single family.

3

Parking Enforcement

Illegal roadside parking on this corridor effectively removes one lane of road capacity. Enforcement — not construction — recovers that lane immediately and at zero cost.

4

Independent Traffic Study

No publicly available, independently reviewed study of this corridor exists. Before spending ₹7,032 crore, commission one — from a university, not a consultant who profits from construction.

We are asking for three things.
